## The Hidden Risks of Standalone Prop Firms

To understand why broker-backed firms are superior, you must first understand how traditional, standalone prop firms operate. Many unverified standalone firms operate on a **B-Book model**. 

In a B-Book model, the prop firm acts as the counterparty to your trades. Your trades are never routed to a live market; they exist purely on a simulated server. 

This creates a massive conflict of interest:

*   **Reliance on Challenge Fees:** These firms rely entirely on the constant influx of new registration fees to survive. If payouts exceed new fees, the firm collapses.
*   **Manufactured Payout Denials:** To protect their own capital, liquidity-strapped firms often resort to arbitrary rule changes or hidden drawdown technicalities to intentionally fail profitable traders.

A broker-backed prop firm eliminates the B-Book conflict of interest. 

Firms like **FXIFY** (partnered with FXPIG) and **Darwinex Zero** (built by Darwinex) operate with the direct financial backing of established financial institutions.

When you trade with these elite firms, you benefit from three critical advantages:

### 1. Genuine Institutional Liquidity

A retail broker processes millions—sometimes billions—of dollars in daily trading volume. 

Because the prop firm is directly tied to the brokerage's liquidity pools, your payout is merely a tiny fraction of their daily turnover. They have the actual financial reserves to pay you on time, every time.

### 2. True STP Execution & Real Capital Allocation

Unlike standalone firms that benefit from your failure, broker-backed firms utilize **Straight-Through Processing (STP)**. 

If you are a consistently profitable trader, the backing broker can easily copy your trades into the live market. Because they are making money alongside you in the real market, they actually want you to succeed.

### 3. Decades of Longevity and Regulation

Retail brokers have spent decades building their brands and acquiring strict regulatory licenses. A multi-million dollar brokerage is not going to ruin its decades-long reputation just to deny a retail trader a payout.
