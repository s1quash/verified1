## Why Professional Traders Focus on the "Majors"

Trading the majors offers a massive advantage: **Liquidity.** Because central banks, hedge funds, and multi-national corporations are constantly exchanging these currencies, the charts are free from the erratic, random spikes found in minor or exotic pairs.

### EUR/USD: The King of Liquidity
The EUR/USD is the most heavily traded financial instrument in the world, representing roughly 24% of all daily forex volume.
*   **The Personality:** It is a smooth, trending pair. It respects support and resistance levels flawlessly and avoids the erratic stop-hunts seen in cross-pairs like GBP/JPY.
*   **When to Trade It:** Maximum liquidity occurs during the **London and New York overlap (1:00 PM to 5:00 PM GMT).** This is when European and American banks are actively trading, pushing the pair into clean, directional moves.

### USD/CAD: The Commodity-Correlated Pair
The USD/CAD (affectionately known as the "Loonie") is uniquely driven by commodity prices.
*   **The Personality:** Because Canada is one of the world's largest exporters of crude oil to the United States, the value of the CAD is heavily correlated to the price of oil (WTI). If oil prices surge, the Canadian Dollar strengthens, which pushes the USD/CAD chart down.
*   **When to Trade It:** The best time to trade USD/CAD is during the New York Session, specifically around major US/Canadian economic data releases or Wednesday’s US Crude Oil Inventory reports.
