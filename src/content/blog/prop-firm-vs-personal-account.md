## The Illusion of Prop Firm Capital

Before comparing the two, we have to debunk a massive industry illusion. When you buy a "$100,000 Prop Firm Challenge," you do not actually have $100,000. If the firm has a **10% Max Drawdown**, you only actually have **$10,000**. The other $90,000 is just cosmetic leverage.

Once you realize that a $100k prop firm account is identical in purchasing power to a $10,000 live account, the transition to personal capital becomes much less intimidating.

## 3 Signs It Is Time to Trade Your Own Money

### 1. You Keep Failing Because of Trailing Drawdowns
A **[trailing drawdown](/blog/static-vs-relative-drawdown/)** tracks your account's highest equity point. You can literally fail an evaluation or lose a funded account while your overall balance is in profit. If trailing drawdowns are ruining your edge, it is time to trade your own money where your stop-loss is the only risk parameter.

### 2. You Suffer from "Payout Anxiety"
If you are purposely avoiding good market setups just to protect a pending payout because you are terrified of hitting a daily loss limit, the prop firm is hindering your potential.

### 3. Your Strategy is Restricted
Prop firms have to protect their capital, which means they enforce strict rules. If you are tired of being forced to close trades on Friday afternoon or being banned from trading during news, a personal account is mandatory.
