## 3 Things Malaysian Gold Traders Must Look For in a Broker

Before opening a live account, ensure your broker meets these three strict criteria for the Malaysian market:

### 1. Raw ECN Spreads (Zero Markup)
Gold spreads can be notoriously high. Many standard "B-Book" brokers markup the XAUUSD spread to 30 or even 50 points (3 to 5 pips). You need an **ECN (Electronic Communication Network)** broker that provides raw liquidity directly from the market, where spreads can drop as low as 0 to 10 points.

### 2. Swap-Free / Islamic Accounts
Malaysian traders heavily favor brokers that offer **Swap-Free (Islamic) accounts**, which comply with Shariah law by removing all overnight interest charges. If you are a swing trader holding gold positions overnight, these accounts are essential to prevent interest from eating your profits.

### 3. Local Funding Methods (FPX)
Dealing with international wire transfers and massive currency conversion fees from **MYR to USD** is a headache. The best brokers in Malaysia support **FPX (Financial Process Exchange)**, allowing instant, fee-free deposits directly from Maybank, CIMB, Public Bank, or via local crypto transfers (USDT).
